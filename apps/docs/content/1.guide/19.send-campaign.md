# Send & Monitor a Campaign

You've built your campaign, chosen your audience, and previewed the result. Now it's time to send it and track how it performs.

## Pre-Send Checklist

::callout{type="warning"}
Run through this checklist before every send. Skipping these steps can hurt your deliverability and sender reputation.

- **Domain verified** -- your sending domain is authenticated with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC
- **Audience hygiene done** -- bounced addresses removed, stale contacts cleaned out
- **Test email sent and reviewed** -- you've checked links, images, and formatting in a real inbox
- **Subject line reviewed** -- clear, concise, and free of spammy language
- **Unsubscribe link present** -- every marketing email must include a working unsubscribe link
::

## Sending Your Campaign

The final step of the campaign wizard is **Review & Send** -- this *is* your last-chance summary screen. It shows a Campaign Summary card with:

- Campaign name
- **From** name and address, plus the Reply-to address if you set one
- Your audience (the Topic or Segment you chose) and the estimated recipient count
- The subject line and the template the campaign uses
- Your A/B test configuration, if you enabled one

Below the summary is a **When to Send** card. Pick **Send Now** and click **Send Campaign** -- delivery begins immediately, with no extra confirmation dialog. Owlat processes your recipient list and starts sending in batches.

::callout{type="tip" title="Send a test first"}
The Review & Send screen also has a **Send Test Email** option. Use it to fire a copy of the campaign to yourself and check links, images, and formatting in a real inbox before you commit to the full send.
::

Sending is blocked until your **From** domain is registered and verified. If it isn't, the When to Send card shows a warning and the Send button stays disabled -- finish DNS verification under **Settings > Domains** first.

## Scheduling a Campaign

If you'd rather send later, choose **Schedule for Later** on the When to Send card:

1. Pick a **date and time** for delivery
2. Optionally tick **Send at recipient's local time** -- Owlat then sends at your chosen clock time in each contact's own timezone. Contacts without a timezone get the email at your selected time.
3. Click **Schedule Campaign**

::callout{type="tip"}
You can change or cancel a scheduled campaign any time before it sends. Open the campaign's edit page and use **Reschedule Campaign** to change the send time, **Unschedule to Edit** to convert it back to a draft so you can edit content, or **Cancel Campaign** to stop it entirely.
::

## Real-Time Dashboard

Once your campaign starts sending, the report page updates in real time with these top-line metrics:

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Sent** | Recipients dispatched to the mail provider |
| **Delivered** | Emails accepted by the recipient's mailbox |
| **Opened** | Recipients who opened your email (with open rate) |
| **Clicked** | Recipients who clicked a link (with click rate) |
| **Bounced** | Emails that couldn't be delivered, split into **Hard** and **Soft** |

You can track these numbers as they come in -- no need to refresh the page.

Below the top-line cards, the report adds an **Opens Over Time** chart, a **Link Click Heatmap** that maps engagement onto your email's links, and **Opened** / **Clicked** tabs listing the individual contacts behind each metric. A/B test campaigns also show a side-by-side variant comparison.

## Interpreting Your Results

Here's a rough guide to what healthy metrics look like:

- **Open rate above 20%** is solid for most industries. Below 15% may signal subject line or deliverability issues.
- **Click rate above 2-3%** means your content is resonating. Higher is better.
- **Bounce rate below 2%** is healthy. Above 5% suggests list quality problems -- watch the Hard vs. Soft split, since hard bounces point to invalid or non-existent addresses.

Spam complaints aren't surfaced as a card on the report, but they still matter: keeping your complaint rate well under 0.1% keeps you in good standing with mailbox providers, and anything above 0.3% is a red flag.

::callout{type="tip"}
Open rates are an approximation. Some email clients block tracking pixels, so your real open rate is likely higher than reported.
::

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

**High bounce rate:** Stop and clean your list. Remove invalid addresses, check for typos in bulk imports, and verify that your list source is reliable. A high bounce rate damages your sender reputation.

**Spike in complaints:** Review your email content and sending frequency. Make sure recipients actually opted in to receive your emails. If complaints exceed 0.3%, pause sending and investigate before your domain gets flagged.

**Low open rate:** Test different subject lines, send at different times, or re-engage inactive subscribers with a targeted win-back campaign. Consider whether your "From" name is recognizable to recipients.

**Links not working:** If you catch broken links after sending, you can't undo a sent email. This is why test emails matter. For future campaigns, double-check every link in your test email.
