This is the fastest path from a blank workspace to a live campaign. If you want to understand all the options and features, start with [Welcome to Owlat](/guide/getting-started) instead.

## Before you start

Make sure you have:

- An active Owlat workspace with your organization set up
- A verified sending domain in **Settings > Technical > Sending Domains** (see [Deliverability](/guide/deliverability) if you haven't done this yet)
- At least a few contacts to send to — you can import from CSV or create them manually in **Audience > Contacts**

## 1. Create a marketing template

1. Open **Mail > Marketing**.
2. Click **New Marketing Template** and give it a name.
3. Build your email using slash commands in the editor. Type `/` in any text block to open the menu:

| Command | What it adds |
| --- | --- |
| `/text` | A paragraph of body text |
| `/h1`, `/h2`, `/h3` | Headings at different sizes |
| `/image` | An image block |
| `/button` | A call-to-action button |
| `/columns` | A multi-column layout |
| `/social` | Social media icon links |

Your [Saved Blocks](/guide/saved-blocks) also appear in this menu — they're listed by their own name under a **Saved** category, so type `/` and start typing the block name to insert one.

To add a personalization variable (like a first name), type `@` or `{{` inside a text block. That opens a separate variable picker where you can pick the field to insert.

4. Save your template when you're happy with the design.

::callout{type="tip"}
Don't worry about perfection on your first template. You can always edit it later, or duplicate it as a starting point for future emails. See [Email Editor](/guide/email-editor) for all available blocks and features.
::

## 2. Create your campaign

Open **Campaigns > All Campaigns** and click **New Campaign**. The campaign wizard walks you through five steps:

::steps

### Basics

Give the campaign a name and set the sender details: the **from name**, the **from email**, and an optional **reply-to** address.

### Audience

Choose who receives the campaign. The audience is exactly one [Topic](/guide/topics) **or** one [Segment](/guide/segments) — pick one or the other. To send to everyone, choose a Topic that contains all of your subscribers. The step shows the eligible recipient count for the audience you select.

### Content

Enter the **email subject** line and select the marketing template you just built. The subject is what recipients see in their inbox.

### A/B Test

Optionally turn on an A/B test to compare two variants — either two subject lines or two templates — across a percentage of your audience, then send the winner to the rest. Leave it off to send a single version to everyone. See [A/B Testing](/guide/ab-testing) for details.

### Review

Verify the campaign summary, send yourself a test email, then choose **Send Now** or **Schedule for Later**.

::

::callout{type="tip"}
The preheader (the snippet shown next to the subject in the inbox) is set on the email template in the [Email Editor](/guide/email-editor), not in the campaign wizard.
::

## 3. Validate before launch

Before hitting send, take a moment to verify:

- **Send a test email** — deliver the email to your own inbox first. Check the layout on both desktop and mobile.
- **Review the subject line** — typos in the subject line are forever.
- **Confirm the sender** — make sure your domain is verified and the "from" address looks professional.
- **Check recipient count** — is the audience size what you expected? Too many or too few contacts might signal a targeting issue.

::callout{type="warning"}
Always send a test email before your first real campaign. It takes 30 seconds and can save you from sending a broken layout or wrong content to your entire list.
::

## 4. Send or schedule

On the **Review** step you have two options:

- **Send Now** — delivery begins immediately after you confirm
- **Schedule for Later** — pick a date and time for delivery. Consider when your audience is most likely to check their email.

The confirm button reads **Send Campaign** or **Schedule Campaign** depending on which option you chose.

When scheduling, you can also enable **Send at recipient's local time**. The campaign then arrives at the chosen local time for each contact; contacts without a known timezone receive it at your scheduled time.

## 5. Review your results

After sending, check how your campaign performed:

- **Sent** — how many emails were sent
- **Delivered** — how many reached the recipient's inbox
- **Open rate** — what percentage of recipients opened your email
- **Click rate** — what percentage clicked a link
- **Bounces** — emails that couldn't be delivered

Find these metrics in the campaign detail page (click into your campaign from **Campaigns > All Campaigns**) or in the aggregate view at **Campaigns > Reports**.

## What to do next

Now that you've sent your first campaign, explore the features that will make your next one even better:

- [A/B Testing](/guide/ab-testing) — test different subject lines to improve open rates
- [Segments](/guide/segments) — target specific groups for more relevant content
- [Automations](/guide/automations) — set up emails that send themselves
- [Saved Blocks](/guide/saved-blocks) — build a library of reusable content
- [Contact Properties](/guide/contact-properties) — personalize emails with custom data
